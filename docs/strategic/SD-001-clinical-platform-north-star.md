# SD-001 — Clinical Platform Architectural North Star

**Status**: Release v1.0

---

## Purpose

Define the strategic purpose of the Clinical Platform.

This document is the architectural reference for every strategic decision concerning the Clinical Platform.

---

## The Problem

Healthcare is collaborative.

Patients are cared for by multiple Practitioners over time.

Between these interventions, clinical continuity is frequently lost.

Information becomes fragmented.

Clinical responsibility becomes unclear.

Clinical knowledge becomes difficult to reconstruct.

The quality and safety of care suffer.

---

## Clinical Platform Mission

The Clinical Platform exists to preserve continuity of care across healthcare practitioners over time.

Everything else exists to support this mission.

---

## Architectural North Star

Enable safe, continuous and collaborative clinical work by preserving clinical responsibility, clinical knowledge and continuity of care.

---

## Strategic Objectives

The Clinical Platform shall always reinforce:

- Clinical Continuity
- Clinical Responsibility
- Clinical Knowledge

---

## What the Clinical Platform is NOT

The Clinical Platform is not:

- an Electronic Health Record;
- a document management system;
- a scheduling application;
- a messaging platform;
- a billing solution.

These capabilities may belong to the MedLink ecosystem but are not part of the Clinical Platform's mission.

---

## Design Principle

The Clinical Platform exists to preserve continuity of care, not to manage clinical documents.

Documents are a consequence of clinical work.

They are not the purpose of the platform.

---

## Scope

This North Star applies exclusively to the Clinical Platform.

Future platforms (Learning, Collaboration, Trust, Conference...) may define their own strategic purpose while remaining aligned with the MedLink ecosystem.
