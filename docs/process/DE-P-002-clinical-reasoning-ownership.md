# DE-P-002 — Clinical Reasoning Belongs to the Practitioner

**Statut** : Accepted
**Date** : 2026-07-13

---

## Principle

Clinical reasoning belongs exclusively to the Practitioner.

---

## Scope

The Domain does not infer, complete, anticipate or replace clinical reasoning.

The Domain records what the Practitioner explicitly chooses to record.

---

## Consequences for Modelling

An Event describes a fact the Practitioner decided to produce.

An Event does not describe how the Practitioner arrived at that decision.

---

## Consequences for AI

AI assistance is preparation.

AI assistance is never reasoning on behalf of the Domain.

The Domain recognises only the Practitioner's explicit act.

---

## Relation to DE-P-001

DE-P-001 defines the boundary from the Domain's perspective:

> Human reasoning is outside the Domain.

DE-P-002 defines it from the Practitioner's perspective:

> Clinical reasoning belongs to the Practitioner.

Both principles are required.

Neither is sufficient alone.
