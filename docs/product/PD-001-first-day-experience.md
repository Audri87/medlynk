# Product Discovery – First Day Experience

## Product Vision

The first experience with MedLink should demonstrate value even when no historical clinical information exists.

The practitioner should never feel blocked by an empty Care Record.

Instead, MedLink should provide an immediate clinical workspace ready for patient care.

---

## First Login

The practitioner discovers:

* today's agenda;
* scheduled patients;
* consultation reasons;
* pending work.

The platform already supports clinical work, even before historical knowledge exists.

---

## First Patient

The Workspace clearly indicates:

> First contact — no previous clinical history available.

This wording is intentional.

It distinguishes the absence of history from missing or lost information.

The practitioner immediately understands the situation.

---

## During Consultation

The Workspace supports clinical reasoning.

The practitioner captures observations, decisions and reasoning naturally.

At the end of the consultation, these become durable Clinical Contributions.

The practitioner realizes that today's work will already improve tomorrow's consultation.

This is the first moment of trust.

---

## End of Day

The practitioner finishes the day knowing:

* every Clinical Activity has been tracked;
* unfinished work remains visible;
* Clinical Knowledge has already begun to emerge;
* tomorrow's consultations will start with more context than today's.

---

## Product Goal

The objective of the first day is not to demonstrate historical knowledge.

The objective is to demonstrate that MedLink continuously builds trustworthy clinical memory through everyday clinical work.

Historical integrations remain important, but they accelerate an already valuable product rather than enabling it.
