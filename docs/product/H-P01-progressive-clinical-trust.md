# Product Hypothesis – H-P01: Progressive Clinical Trust

## Status

Active

---

# Context

The domain model of MedLink is now considered sufficiently stable.

Clinical Activities produce Clinical Contributions.

Clinical Contributions progressively build the patient's Care Record.

The remaining uncertainty no longer concerns the domain model.

It concerns the practitioner's first experience using MedLink.

---

# Hypothesis

**A practitioner who completes one Clinical Activity in MedLink should immediately perceive enough value to choose MedLink again for the next Clinical Activity, even when no historical clinical information exists.**

This perceived value must emerge from the practitioner's daily work, not from historical data imports or external integrations.

---

# Why this matters

MedLink adopts a Progressive Adoption Strategy.

Practitioners are never required to migrate their historical data before benefiting from the platform.

Therefore, the first Clinical Activity becomes the most important moment of product adoption.

If this experience fails, historical integrations become irrelevant because practitioners will never reach them.

---

# Success Criteria

After the practitioner's first working day, the following statements should naturally emerge:

* "I found my notes easily."
* "I understood where I was."
* "Nothing important was lost."
* "I know exactly what remains to do."
* "The next consultation will be easier."
* **"Tomorrow, I will use MedLink again."**

The last statement is considered the primary success criterion.

---

# Failure Criteria

The hypothesis is considered falsified if practitioners consistently report:

* "I would rather return to my previous software."
* "The first consultation required too much effort."
* "I do not yet understand the benefit."
* "The empty Care Record makes the product feel unfinished."

---

# Sprint 1 Objective

Sprint 1 is **not** intended to maximize the number of delivered features.

Its objective is to maximize confidence after the practitioner's first Clinical Activity.

Every implementation decision should therefore answer one question:

> **Does this increase the probability that the practitioner chooses MedLink again tomorrow?**

If the answer is no, the feature should probably not belong to Sprint 1.

---

# Validation

This hypothesis will be validated through qualitative interviews and usability testing with:

* General practitioners
* Psychologists
* Nurses

The purpose of these sessions is not to evaluate the domain model.

The purpose is to determine whether the first day experience creates sufficient trust for progressive adoption.
